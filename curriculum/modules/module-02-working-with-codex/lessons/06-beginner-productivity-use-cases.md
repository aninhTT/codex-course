# Lesson 2.06: Beginner Productivity Use Cases

## Learn

Codex becomes more compelling when it is tied to familiar work. In this course, that means summaries, action items, templates, planning help, and simple workflow support rather than advanced engineering tasks.

A good beginner use case is small, familiar, and easy to check. If you cannot tell whether Codex did a good job, shrink the task until you can review the result confidently.

## Practice

Try this in Codex:

```text
Based on what you know about my role, list 6 beginner-friendly ways I could use Codex in day-to-day work. Focus on practical productivity tasks, not coding-heavy examples. For each idea, tell me what file, folder, or artifact I could use to keep the task grounded and easy to review.
```

**Success criteria:** Codex suggests grounded ideas that clearly connect to the current workspace, and the learner can identify at least two that feel relevant immediately.
