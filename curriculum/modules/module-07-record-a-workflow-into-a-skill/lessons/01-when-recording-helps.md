# Lesson 7.01: When Recording Helps

## Learn

In Module 4, you learned to interview a workflow before turning it into a skill. Record & Replay is a different capture method: you show Codex the workflow on your computer, then refine the skill it drafts from what it observed.

Recording is useful when the workflow is repetitive, stable, and easier to demonstrate than describe. Good examples include downloading the same kind of report, filing a recurring request, creating a correctly configured issue, or preparing a standard workspace.

Recording is not a good first choice when the workflow includes secrets, sensitive personal data, lots of judgment calls, unstable screens, or one-off exploration. If the path is fuzzy, interview the workflow first.

## Practice

Try this in Codex:

```text
Help me choose one workflow that would be a good candidate for Record & Replay. Use what I built in Modules 3 and 4 as context. Compare 2 or 3 possible workflows and recommend the one that is most repetitive, stable, safe to demonstrate, and easy to verify.
```

**Success criteria:** The learner chooses one recording candidate and can explain why it is stable enough to show Codex.
